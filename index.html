Featured metrics

SMOG Calculator

SMOG estimates the years of education needed to understand a piece of writing. A widely used formula, SMOG was published in 1969 before computers so Dr McLaughlin provided both a detailed version and a simplified one (WordsCount used the more accurate, detailed one). The simple version says to count the words of 3 or more syllables in 3 10-sentence samples, estimate the count's square root, and add 3.

CLEAR

CLEAR is a tool to help writers keep it simple. CLEAR estimates the grade level of a submitted text by giving every word and sentence a grade between 1 and 20, corresponding to the years of education an adult needs to understand them easily. The text is returned color coded to show levels of difficulty. CLEAR also provides synonyms for hard words. Soon to be validated, CLEAR will provide the first readability formula normed specifically for adults.

Syllable Scythe

This counts syllables. We think it is the most accurate online tool available. Most tools programmatically compute syllables, but WordsCount's accuracy comes from a dictionary: we look up every word whose syllable count has been verified. For those words not in the dictionary, we revert to an updated algorithm.

Stat Mule

There are numerous metrics calculated by our statistical analysis engine. Most of these measures are exact and what they are is obvious, but the hardest to get right is the sentence count. If you submit a passage that results in an incorrect sentence count, please submit the passage with the results so we can improve our service.
